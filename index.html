About me

I have to admit, I have a BsC degree in Wood Industry Engineering.

So... What's the deal? How come you have that degree, aren't you that passionate about development after all?

Ohh, not that fast, we're getting there. In 2018 I realized that what I am learning in the uni wasn't necessarily the field I want to have a career in and I was a bit blue about that (having an allergy to dust wasn't helping much either). So I started to brainstorm what can be done. There are a lot of opportunities, but I remembered how much I liked that little website creation class in secondary school. I thought I gave it a try, let's see if I like it or not.

Why did I choose the wood industry to begin with?

Want to know why? Because woodworking is freaking badass, that is why. It was a good enough reason for Elon Musk to produce that Cybertruck, so I am not ashamed about it either. (Little did I knew back then that it wasn't specifically about woodworking, but that is another story) And I had a lot of other interests too. At one point I was a hobby actor, did music production, tinkered with electric circuits, built electrical guitars from scratch, and made wooden jewelry.

I started with mapping out the areas I need to learn and there were plenty of them. As I am learning and moving forward a lot of things are clearer and clearer and it just amazes me how much more can be known and done in this field. It is exciting as hell.

What am I learning exactly?

Since 2018 I have tried to deepen my knowledge in vanilla HTML CSS and JavaScript as much as I could with varying intensity. Now I feel confident enough to learn and make projects with React, databases, and back-end (Machine learning, Linux, networks are pretty damn interesting too).
